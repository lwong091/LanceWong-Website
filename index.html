Lance Wong

Psychology

My name is Lance Wong, 18 years old and I am currently a freshman psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. I am from San Jose in the Bay Area, graduating in 2022 from Saratoga High School. I have lived there all my life, though I have traveled abroad to Europe and Asia on multiple occassions. I pride myself on my people skills, patience, trustworthiness, sensibility, creativity, organization, drive, adaptability, and flair.

At the moment, I work as a research assistant in the Happiness and Well-Being Lab, under Dr. Sonja Lyubomirksy. In my spare time, I am an avid writer, primarily specializing in creative and descriptive writing of various genres. Furthermore, I am an accomplished cellist, playing for nearly 10 years, and having served on the Saratoga Philharmonic Strings Orchestra as principal cellist, and orchestra manager for 3 years. I have performed various concerts and solo recitals in my lifetime, including traveling abroad to Central Europe in some of the most prestigious music halls in the world. Leaning further into my creative side, I also have experience in art, mostly delving into painting, though I have tested a plethora of mediums. This includes but is not limited to acrylics, oils, watercolors, charcoal, graphite, wire art, mixed media, potraits, still life, landscapes, and linoleum prints. Despite this, I am very much an outdoorsy person. On the weekends, I love to hike or rock climb, and am also a member of Riverside Surging Dragons, the University of California, Riverside's Dragon Boat team.

While I do not know what exactly I wish to pursue yet, I have a formal interest in happiness and its associated facets, and where happiness psychology intersects with developmental and social psychology. I also hold a great passion for teaching, and working with children, having taught locally and abroad over the course of the past few years. In regards to my future educational goals, I may pursue a double major in business administration to put my people skills to use, and perhaps pursue a Master's in graduate school for psychology, since I already operate in research.
